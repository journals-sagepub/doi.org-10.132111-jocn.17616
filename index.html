The Effect of Prolanis Services Plus Home Visits on the Quality of Life of Patients with Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus in the Working Area of Jatinegara Health Center, East Jakarta

ABSTRACT

Aims

This study aimed to analyze the effect of Prolanis services strengthened by structured home visits on the quality of life of patients with type 2 diabetes mellitus in the working area of Jatinegara Primary Health Center, East Jakarta.

Design

The study employed a quasi-experimental pre-test–post-test design with a control group. The research was conducted in two stages: development of a structured home visit module using a research and development approach, followed by implementation of the intervention over an eight-week period.

Methods

A total of 60 respondents were divided into an intervention group (n = 30) and a control group (n = 30). The intervention group received Prolanis services combined with four structured home visits, while the control group received routine Prolanis services only. Quality of life was measured using the WHOQOL-BREF questionnaire. Data were analyzed using the Wilcoxon test and Mann–Whitney test.

Results

There was no significant difference in baseline quality of life between the intervention and control groups (p = 0.818). Following the intervention, both groups demonstrated significant improvements in quality of life (p < 0.001). The intervention group showed an increase in mean quality of life score from 74.73 to 96.10 (Δ = 21.37), while the control group increased from 73.07 to 90.47 (Δ = 17.40). The difference in improvement between groups was statistically significant (p = 0.010).

Conclusion

Strengthening Prolanis services through structured home visits resulted in greater improvements in quality of life among patients with type 2 diabetes mellitus compared with routine services alone. Structured home visits should be integrated into Prolanis as a sustainable strategy for patient monitoring and continuous health education. Future studies are recommended to involve larger sample sizes and longer intervention periods to assess long-term outcomes.

INTRODUCTION

Type 2 diabetes mellitus is a chronic disease that requires continuous management and significantly affects patients’ quality of life. The Chronic Disease Management Program (Prolanis) is a primary health care strategy designed to support disease control and improve health outcomes among patients with chronic illnesses. However, the implementation of Prolanis has not fully addressed individual patient needs, particularly in terms of continuous monitoring, personalized education, and support for self-management. Structured home visits have the potential to strengthen Prolanis services by providing direct patient engagement, individualized counseling, and closer follow-up. Therefore, this study aimed to analyze the effect of Prolanis services strengthened by structured home visits on the quality of life of patients with type 2 diabetes mellitus in the working area of Jatinegara Primary Health Center, East Jakarta.

METHODS

This study was conducted in two stages. The first stage involved the development of a structured home visit module using a research and development approach. The second stage employed a quasi-experimental pre-test–post-test design with a control group. A total of 60 respondents were recruited and divided equally into an intervention group (n = 30) and a control group (n = 30). The intervention group received routine Prolanis services combined with four structured home visits over an eight-week period, while the control group received routine Prolanis services only. Quality of life was measured using the WHOQOL-BREF questionnaire. Statistical analyses were performed using the Wilcoxon test and Mann–Whitney test to evaluate changes within and between groups.
